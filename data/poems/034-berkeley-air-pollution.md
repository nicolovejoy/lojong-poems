# Don't transfer the ox's load to the cow

*Slogan 34 — after a study by Jason G. Su, UC Berkeley School of Public Health*

Eight hundred and fifty monitoring stations.
Weather and traffic reports
from 2012 to 2019.

Daily air pollution maps
for Fresno, Los Angeles, Sacramento.

A nearly block-by-block picture.

~~Situated far from~~ Kept away from
high-income neighborhoods.
Freeways, ports, railyards.

The lower-income areas saw the biggest decline
because they had the most pollution to begin with.

Collected as little as once a year —
from monitoring stations
that are miles away from people's homes.

They don't put rail yards in Piedmont.

Whose lungs were the first monitors?

---

**Source:** "New method for mapping air pollution reveals disproportionate burden in disadvantaged communities," UC Berkeley Public Health, September 11, 2024
**Slogan:** 34 — Don't transfer the ox's load to the cow
**Intervention:** "situated far from" → "kept away from"
